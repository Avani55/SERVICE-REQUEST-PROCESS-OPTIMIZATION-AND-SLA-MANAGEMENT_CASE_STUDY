# SERVICE-REQUEST-PROCESS-OPTIMIZATION-AND-SLA-MANAGEMENT_CASE_STUDY

India’s BPO and IT-enabled services industry employs more than **5.4 million professionals** and supports global customers across industries such as telecom, banking, e-commerce, and technology.

Cities like Gurugram and Noida in the National Capital Region (NCR) have become major hubs for customer support operations, where thousands of professionals work every day to resolve customer concerns and ensure service continuity.

While working closely with the telecommunications process, I had the opportunity to observe the complete service request lifecycle — from the moment a customer raises a complaint to ticket creation, escalation handling, and final resolution.

Through this experience, I noticed several operational challenges that are common in large-scale customer support environments:

• SLA breaches
• Repeat customer calls
• Inefficient escalation handling
• Ticket routing delays
• Customer frustration due to delayed resolution

To understand these issues from a process perspective, I conducted a **Technical Business Analyst case study on Service Request Process Optimization and SLA Management**.

As part of this project, I worked on:
• Analyzing the service request lifecycle
• Identifying process gaps and operational inefficiencies
• Designing As-Is and To-Be process models
• Defining key operational KPIs such as SLA Compliance, First Call Resolution (FCR), Average Handling Time (AHT), and Escalation Rate
• Proposing a rule-based monitoring mechanism for proactive escalation management

One important insight from this study is that across every corporate environment — whether it is a product company, service organization, technology team, or support center — the core objective remains the same:

**Solving customer problems efficiently.**

From developers and technical teams to Business Analysts, Data Analysts, stakeholders, and customer support professionals — every role ultimately contributes to improving the customer experience. 


# Project Title

**Service Request Process Optimization and SLA Management**

## Overview

Organizations often receive a large number of service requests from customers through multiple channels such as email, web portals, and support systems. Inefficient ticket routing can lead to delays, incorrect assignment of requests, increased resolution times, and violations of Service Level Agreements (SLAs). These issues negatively impact customer satisfaction, operational efficiency, and business performance.

This project focuses on analyzing the existing service request management process, identifying bottlenecks that cause SLA breaches, and proposing optimized workflows to improve ticket handling efficiency and service delivery.

## Objectives

* Analyze the complete service request lifecycle from ticket creation to closure.
* Identify patterns and root causes of SLA breaches.
* Improve ticket routing and assignment processes.
* Reduce repeat complaints and recurring service issues.
* Enhance overall customer service efficiency and response times.

## Key Deliverables

### 1. Business Requirement Document (BRD)

A comprehensive document capturing business goals, stakeholder requirements, project scope, assumptions, constraints, and success criteria.

### 2. System Requirement Specification (SRS)

A detailed specification of functional and non-functional requirements, system behavior, workflows, business rules, and reporting needs.

### 3. As-Is and To-Be Process Models

* **As-Is Process Model:** Current ticket management workflow highlighting existing inefficiencies.
* **To-Be Process Model:** Optimized workflow demonstrating improved ticket routing, escalation handling, and SLA compliance.

### 4. SLA KPI Dashboard

An Excel-based dashboard providing visibility into:

* SLA Compliance Rate
* Average Resolution Time
* First Response Time
* Escalation Rate
* Repeat Complaint Rate
* Ticket Volume by Category
* Agent Performance Metrics

## Expected Business Benefits

* Improved SLA compliance and service quality
* Faster ticket resolution and response times
* Better workload distribution among support teams
* Reduction in customer complaints and escalations
