# SERVICE-REQUEST-PROCESS-OPTIMIZATION-AND-SLA-MANAGEMENT_CASE_STUDY

India’s BPO and IT-enabled services industry employs more than **5.4 million professionals** and supports global customers across industries such as telecom, banking, e-commerce, and technology.

Cities like Gurugram and Noida in the National Capital Region (NCR) have become major hubs for customer support operations, where thousands of professionals work every day to resolve customer concerns and ensure service continuity.

While working closely with the telecommunications process, I had the opportunity to observe the complete service request lifecycle — from the moment a customer raises a complaint to ticket creation, escalation handling, and final resolution.

Through this experience, I noticed several operational challenges that are common in large-scale customer support environments:

• SLA breaches
• Repeat customer calls
• Inefficient escalation handling
• Ticket routing delays
• Customer frustration due to delayed resolution

To understand these issues from a process perspective, I conducted a **Technical Business Analyst case study on Service Request Process Optimization and SLA Management**.

As part of this project, I worked on:
• Analyzing the service request lifecycle
• Identifying process gaps and operational inefficiencies
• Designing As-Is and To-Be process models
• Defining key operational KPIs such as SLA Compliance, First Call Resolution (FCR), Average Handling Time (AHT), and Escalation Rate
• Proposing a rule-based monitoring mechanism for proactive escalation management

One important insight from this study is that across every corporate environment — whether it is a product company, service organization, technology team, or support center — the core objective remains the same:

**Solving customer problems efficiently.**

From developers and technical teams to Business Analysts, Data Analysts, stakeholders, and customer support professionals — every role ultimately contributes to improving the customer experience.

This case study is my attempt to analyze a real operational process and explore how structured business analysis can help optimize service delivery and operational efficiency.
